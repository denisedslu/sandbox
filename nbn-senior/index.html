It’s been real, Northwestern

By Shaunacy Ferro

I’m finding it difficult to say goodbye to Northwestern. Not because I know I’ll miss it, but because I worry I won’t. There came a point in the middle of Winter Quarter, when I was away from campus on JR, toying with the idea of cutting and running, leaving my Slavic minor incomplete and never returning. But the idea of not being a student anymore scared the shit out of me. I guess it still does, though coming back has made me realize I’m not terribly good at student life anymore. I like my weekends free and my bedtimes early. I can barely motivate myself to go to my two classes. I feel old.

What am I coming away with after four years and $150,000? A certain haggard look. An awareness of my limitations (and what sleep-deprivation hallucinations look like). A random smattering of incomplete knowledge of Russian history, Latin American politics and Middle English. Best friends. A promise of future employment.

By all calculations, Northwestern has done me well. Yet I find it hard to look back on my time spent here and think, aha, I’ve been doing it right.

I never regretted my choice to go to Northwestern. I used to walk around campus, as a doe-eyed freshman seeing a real Midwestern Fall for the first time and feeling so satisfied that I hadn’t made a mistake in coming here. And I still think that. I can’t imagine having gone anywhere else. So where is my sense of closure? I feel terrified to leave this place, even though in truth I’ve been halfway out the door since the first day of spring classes.

I wonder what I would do differently, if I could. Maybe nothing. The couple quarters I spent proving my own clumsiness on the Frisbee team? I’ll never be an athlete, but in those months I was stronger than I’ll likely ever be again. Thinking about the miserable year I spent hating my life as a member of a mismanaged exec board still gives me heartburn, but it will probably give me fodder for job interviews for years to come. The hours I poured into the back end of this website took away from the things I was actually paying to learn, but taught me more than Medill ever could.

Perhaps I’ll regret all the times I avoided saying "Hi" to acquaintances on Sheridan Road or all the people I thought were cool but never earnestly tried to befriend. I should probably regret the time I spent holed up in my bed watching Netflix. I might regret living in a "brothel."

I guess I regret that I don’t feel the impending loss of my identity as a Northwestern student more deeply, maybe because my ties to campus have thinned over the years, and I haven’t worked hard enough to strengthen them. But the experiences that have made my Northwestern experience great won’t disappear the moment I take off that sweaty graduation gown. I’d like to think I’ve learned some stuff, even if those things weren’t on my syllabi.

So see you later, Northwestern. It was great, except the times when it wasn’t. In a few years, when life stretches endlessly before me with nary a spring break in sight, I’m sure I’ll miss you.
